AI
Dr. Alex Okonkwo AI/ML Lead

Jordan, the Golden Hour concept is perfect for what I've been prototyping. Let me walk through the technical architecture for a mobile-first workflow:

Mobile Upload + AI Pipeline
  • Step 1: Photographer connects SD card to iPhone via Lightning/USB-C reader
  • Step 2: App performs on-device "quick cull" using Core ML—identifies obvious rejects (blurry, eyes closed)
  • Step 3: Uploads "keepers" to Imagen cloud over WiFi/cellular (background upload)
  • Step 4: Cloud AI begins processing with their Personal AI Profile
  • Step 5: First 10 "hero shots" are processed in priority queue (5 min)
  • Step 6: Photographer can share these via WhatsApp/iMessage as sneak peeks
  • Step 7: Full gallery processes overnight, ready for morning review

The key innovation here is progressive processing. We don't need to wait for the full gallery. We can use our AI to identify the most "shareable" shots—good composition, emotional moments, ring shots, first kiss—and prioritize those.

I've been training a "Shareability Score" model on the photos that users export first, share on social media, or mark as favorites. Early results show 87% accuracy in predicting which photos will be selected for sneak peeks.

Imagine: You finish shooting a wedding at 10pm. By 10:30pm, while driving home, your phone buzzes: "5 sneak peeks ready to share." You forward them to the couple before you even get home. That's magic.

— Technical Vision Statement

Part 3: MVP Definition
UX
Jordan Rivera UX Researcher

For MVP scoping, I always start with the Job-to-be-Done. Based on our research, the primary JTBD for mobile is:

When I'm away from my computer after a shoot, I want to start the editing process and share quick previews, so that I can reduce my anxiety, impress my clients, and feel productive.

— Primary JTBD for Imagen Mobile

Notice it's not "edit photos on my phone." Nobody wants to do detailed color grading on a 6-inch screen. The job is about starting the process and sharing previews.

MVP Feature Set (Tightly Scoped)
  • Core Feature 1: Upload photos from SD card/camera roll to Imagen cloud
  • Core Feature 2: Monitor AI processing status in real-time
  • Core Feature 3: Preview AI-edited results (swipe through gallery)
  • Core Feature 4: Quick share to WhatsApp/iMessage/Instagram (1-tap)
  • Core Feature 5: Push notifications ("Your gallery is ready!")

What we're explicitly NOT building in MVP:

  • Manual editing tools (sliders, curves, etc.)
  • Full culling workflow (just basic star/reject)
  • Client gallery delivery
  • Album design
  • Invoicing/contracts

The MVP is a "remote control for your AI"—not a mobile editing suite.

Upload Reliability Requirements

  • Cellular vs WiFi-only toggle
  • Storage space warnings

Part 6: Rollout Strategy
PM

For rollout, I'm proposing a phased approach that balances speed with learning:

Rollout Plan

Launch Marketing:
