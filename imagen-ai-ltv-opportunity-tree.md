# Opportunity Solution Tree: Increase Photographer LTV by 40%

## Framework: Teresa Torres' Continuous Discovery

This OST follows Teresa Torres' methodology from "Continuous Discovery Habits":
- **Outcome**: Business goal we want to achieve
- **Opportunities**: Customer needs, pain points, desires (NOT solutions)
- **Solutions**: Ideas that address opportunities
- **Experiments**: Tests to validate solutions will work

## üí° OPPORTUNITIES

Opportunities are framed as **customer needs, pain points, and desires** discovered through research of 52 photographer problems across Wedding, Event, and Portrait segments.

### Opportunity 1: "I waste hours on tasks Imagen doesn't help with"

**Customer verbatim:** "Imagen saves me so much time on editing, but then I spend hours on albums, galleries, and invoicing. I wish it did more."

**Evidence:**
- 66% of photographer workflow problems remain unsolved
- Photographers use 3-5 additional tools alongside Imagen
- Post-editing tasks take 10-15 hours per job
- Top unsolved: Album design (3-6 hrs), Gallery delivery (30-60 min), Payment chasing (ongoing)

**Segments affected:** All (Wedding, Event, Portrait)

---

### Opportunity 2: "I can't deliver photos fast enough for my clients"

**Customer verbatim:** "Corporate clients want photos within 24 hours. Even with Imagen editing, I can't tag, organize, and deliver that fast."

**Evidence:**
- Event photographers face 24-48 hour delivery expectations
- Manual attendee tagging takes 2-4 hours per event
- No real-time upload/delivery workflow exists
- Marketing teams want photos during the event

**Segments affected:** Primarily Event, some Wedding

---

### Opportunity 3: "Album design takes forever and I dread it"

**Customer verbatim:** "I love shooting weddings but hate spending 4-6 hours laying out albums. It's tedious and clients always want revisions."

**Evidence:**
- 95% of wedding photographers offer albums
- Album design takes 3-6 hours per wedding
- Revision cycles add 1-2 more hours
- Photos already in Imagen after editing - natural extension

**Segments affected:** Primarily Wedding

---

### Opportunity 4: "My portrait retouching still takes forever"

**Customer verbatim:** "Imagen handles color grading great, but I still spend 20-40 minutes per image on skin retouching, body shaping, and background fixes."

**Evidence:**
- Advanced retouching takes 20-40 min per image
- Portrait photographers deliver 20-50 retouched images per session
- Total retouching time: 6-33 hours per session
- Clients expect Instagram-level retouching

**Segments affected:** Primarily Portrait

---

### Opportunity 5: "I lose track of clients and miss follow-ups"

**Customer verbatim:** "I use HoneyBook for contracts, Pixieset for galleries, QuickBooks for invoices, Gmail for communication. Nothing talks to each other."

**Evidence:**
- Photographers use 3-5 separate tools for client management
- No unified view of client journey
- Leads fall through cracks
- Payment collection is awkward and delayed

**Segments affected:** All

---

### Opportunity 6: "I want to look more professional to clients"

**Customer verbatim:** "I wish I had one beautiful portal where clients could see their photos, sign contracts, pay invoices, and communicate with me."

**Evidence:**
- Fragmented experience hurts perceived professionalism
- Clients confused by multiple platforms
- Photographers embarrassed by cobbled-together workflow
- Competitors (studios) have more polished client experience

**Segments affected:** All

---

### Opportunity 7: "I can't keep up during busy season"

**Customer verbatim:** "May through October I'm drowning. I shoot every weekend and editing stacks up. I need to work faster or I'll burn out."

**Evidence:**
- Wedding season creates 4-5x workload
- Portrait photographers overwhelmed during holiday mini-session season
- Backlog causes delivery delays and client complaints
- Burnout is #1 reason photographers leave industry

**Segments affected:** All, especially Wedding and Portrait

---

### Opportunity 8: "I need my recurring clients to look consistent"

**Customer verbatim:** "I shoot quarterly events for the same corporate client. They expect the exact same look every time, but I can never quite match it."

**Evidence:**
- Event photographers have recurring corporate clients
- Brand consistency is critical for corporate work
- Currently no way to save client-specific profiles
- Inconsistency risks losing recurring contracts

**Segments affected:** Primarily Event, some Portrait (returning families)

## üß™ EXPERIMENTS

Experiments validate that solutions will actually work before full investment.

### Experiment Framework

For each solution, we design experiments using:
- **Assumption**: What we believe to be true
- **Test**: How we'll validate it
- **Metric**: What we'll measure
- **Success criteria**: What result means we proceed

---

### Priority Experiments (Next Quarter)

#### Experiment 1: Gallery Delivery Demand

**Assumption:** Photographers will pay extra for integrated gallery delivery and reduce tool fragmentation.

**Test:** Survey + fake door test
- Survey 200 photographers on gallery pain points
- Add "Coming Soon: Imagen Galleries" to dashboard
- Measure click-through and waitlist signups

**Metrics:**
- Survey: % who use 2+ gallery platforms
- Fake door: Click-through rate, waitlist conversion

**Success criteria:**
- 70%+ use multiple gallery tools
- 15%+ click-through on fake door
- 500+ waitlist signups in 30 days

---

#### Experiment 2: Album Design Value

**Assumption:** Wedding photographers will pay $20+/month more for AI album design.

**Test:** Concierge MVP
- Manually create AI-style album layouts for 20 photographers
- Charge $25 per album as one-time fee
- Measure satisfaction and willingness to subscribe

**Metrics:**
- Completion rate
- Time saved (self-reported)
- NPS score
- Willingness to pay monthly

**Success criteria:**
- 80%+ satisfaction
- 60%+ time savings reported
- 50%+ would pay $20/month

---

#### Experiment 3: Facial Recognition Accuracy

**Assumption:** AI can accurately tag event attendees with 90%+ accuracy.

**Test:** Technical spike
- Build proof-of-concept with 1,000 event photos
- Test across different lighting conditions
- Measure accuracy with known attendee list

**Metrics:**
- True positive rate (correct tags)
- False positive rate (wrong tags)
- Processing time per photo

**Success criteria:**
- 90%+ true positive rate
- <5% false positive rate
- <2 seconds per photo

---

#### Experiment 4: Portrait Retouching Quality

**Assumption:** AI retouching can match manual quality at 90% time savings.

**Test:** Blind comparison
- AI retouch 50 portrait images
- Have same images manually retouched
- Show both to 20 photographers (blind)
- Ask which they'd deliver to client

**Metrics:**
- Preference rate (AI vs manual)
- Quality score (1-10)
- Time comparison

**Success criteria:**
- 70%+ find AI acceptable for delivery
- Average quality score 7+
- 85%+ time savings

---

#### Experiment 5: Payment Integration Adoption

**Assumption:** Photographers will use integrated payments if it reduces collection time.

**Test:** Beta pilot
- Build Stripe integration MVP
- Launch with 30 photographers
- Track payment collection metrics

**Metrics:**
- Adoption rate (% who enable)
- Collection time (days to payment)
- Revenue per photographer

**Success criteria:**
- 60%+ enable payments
- 50%+ faster collection
- $10+ ARPU increase

3. **Validate before building** - Experiments test assumptions before major investment

5. **Continuous discovery** - This tree should be updated weekly as we learn from experiments
