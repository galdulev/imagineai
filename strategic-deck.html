Strategic Business Goal

From "Tool" ‚Üí "One Stop Shop"

Expand Share of Workflow
North Star Metric: Increase the number of workflow stages an average photographer finishes within the Imagen AI ecosystem within 6 months by 20%
Understanding Exit Points
To achieve this goal, we must understand when photographers exit the Imagen ecosystem:
1. Exit for Different Tools
Photographers leave Imagen to use competitors (Pixieset, ShootProof, Pic-Time) for delivery, CRM tools (HoneyBook, Studio Ninja) for booking, or other specialized tools for specific workflow stages.
2. Exit for Manual Work
Photographers exit to perform tasks manually that we could solve: exporting photos, uploading to galleries, organizing folders, client communication, social media posting, payment collection, contract management.
3. Frictions We Can Solve
Every exit point represents a friction or opportunity. By identifying these exit points, we can build solutions that keep photographers within the Imagen ecosystem for more workflow stages.

Synthesis: 5 "Big Truths"
1. The Editing High Ends Abruptly
Photographers love editing, then thrown into worst part: manual gallery building, client requests, marketing. This is where burnout happens.

2. Clients Live on Mobile, Tools Don't
80%+ of gallery views happen on mobile (client behavior), but current gallery platforms are desktop-first.

3. Revenue Leakage is Embarrassing
Photographers want to upsell but feel awkward asking directly. Need automatic, built-in monetization.

4. Marketing is a Burden
Photographers are creatives, not marketers. Spend equal or more time on marketing than shooting.

5. Tool Fragmentation Creates Friction
Every tool switch is a friction point. The more stages we own, the more valuable we become.

Hypothesis & Solutions

The Hypothesis
Because today, after photographers finish editing‚Äîthe part they love‚Äîthey are thrown into the most painful, time-consuming, uncreative, unpaid, and chaotic part of their job: building galleries manually, organizing thousands of photos into folders, dealing with guests who constantly request "photos of me," watching their work get screenshot in low quality, losing potential revenue because they feel uncomfortable upselling, and spending more time being marketers and customer-support reps than actual photographers. This is the point in the workflow where photographers burn out, lose money, lose brand visibility, and feel the most frustration.
What We Will Build
A mobile-first AI gallery that removes all of that pain. It automatically segments the wedding into story-based sections, recognizes faces so guests can self-serve without bothering the photographer, presents images beautifully on mobile (where everyone actually views them), protects the photographer's brand, and includes built-in, automatic monetization‚Äîprints, downloads, tips, parent albums‚Äîso photographers earn more without awkward sales conversations. This turns the worst part of their job into the easiest, transforming delivery, sharing, and client interactions into an effortless, premium experience that also grows their business passively.
Four Solutions
1. Automatic Story-Based Segmentation
AI organizes into ceremony, reception, portraits, etc.

2. Face Recognition for Self-Service
Guests find "photos of me" without photographer help

3. Mobile-First Beautiful Presentation
Optimized for mobile viewing (where clients actually are)

4. Built-In Automatic Monetization
Prints, downloads, albums, tips (no awkward sales)
