Strategic Business Goal

From "Tool" ‚Üí "Operating System"

Current State
Imagen AI is a powerful tool for specific tasks (culling & editing). Currently owns the middle of the workflow (Cull + Edit).
Target State
Imagen AI becomes the operating system for photographers' entire workflow. To become a "one-stop-shop," we must own the end of the workflow.
Selected Next Capability: Integrated Client Delivery
Context: After Imagen edits photos, photographers must export and upload to competitors (Pixieset, ShootProof) to deliver to clients.

Opportunity: By building Delivery, we remove the "Export & Upload" friction, directly fulfilling the mission of saving time.

The North Star Metric

Weekly Completed Workflows (WCW)

WCW
Weekly Completed Workflows
Definition
A unique project (Session/Wedding) that is processed in Imagen (Edited) AND successfully published/delivered to an end-client via Imagen's gallery within the last 7 days.
06

Why WCW is Superior

Analytical Reasoning from Product Interviews

1. Alignment with Mission
"Photos Edited" is a vanity metric. If a user edits 1,000 photos but then spends 2 hours exporting and uploading to Pixieset, we've failed the mission of "giving time back." WCW measures true workflow completion.
2. Measures True Value
Revenue alone doesn't capture workflow completion. WCW measures end-to-end value delivery‚Äîthe moment when photographer AND client both benefit.
3. Predicts Retention
Users who complete workflows are 3x more likely to use additional features and stay with Imagen. WCW is a leading indicator of product-market fit.
4. Actionable & Measurable
Clear binary outcome (yes/no per project). Product teams can directly impact this metric. Enables A/B testing and clear goal-setting.

Insights from Research

5 "Big Truths" About Wedding Photographers

1. The Editing High Ends Abruptly
Photographers love the creative part (editing with Imagen), then they're thrown into the worst part: manual gallery building, client requests, marketing. This is where burnout happens.
2. Clients Live on Mobile, Tools Don't
80%+ of gallery views happen on mobile (client behavior), but current gallery platforms are desktop-first. Photographers need tools that serve mobile-first clients.
3. Revenue Leakage is Embarrassing
Photographers want to upsell (albums, prints, products) but feel awkward asking directly. They need automatic, built-in monetization that doesn't feel salesy.
4. Marketing is a Burden
Photographers are creatives, not marketers. They spend equal or more time on marketing than shooting. They need automated marketing that leverages their work.
