2 How I Would Perform the Research

A systematic discovery process that combines quantitative data mining, qualitative user research, and competitive intelligence to identify and prioritize opportunities.

üîç Phase 1: Discovery Research (Weeks 1-3)

Internal Data Mining
  • Feature usage analytics: What do users do before/after Imagen?
  • Churn analysis: Why do users leave? What's missing?
  • Support ticket themes: What do users ask for?
  • NPS verbatims: Thematic analysis of feedback
  • Integration patterns: What other tools are connected?
Story-Based Interviews
15-20 interviews focused on stories, not opinions:
  • "Tell me about the last wedding you shot..."
  • "What happened next?"
  • "What was hard about that?"
  • "What did you try before that?"

Goal: Collect specific stories, not generalizations

Competitive Intelligence
  • Feature matrix vs. competitors (Pixieset, ShootProof, Pic-Time, Aftershoot)
  • Pricing & bundling analysis
  • Review mining: G2, Capterra, FB groups
  • Acquisition signals: What's being bought?
Community Listening
  • Facebook: Rising Tide Society, Photography Business groups
  • Reddit: r/WeddingPhotography, r/photography
  • YouTube comments on workflow videos

Look for: "I wish..." statements, tool recommendations, pricing sensitivity

üìã Phase 3: Assumption Mapping (Week 5)

For each top opportunity, map the riskiest assumptions across four dimensions:

Will they want it?
  • Photographers want this in same tool as editing
  • Current solutions are painful enough to switch
  • Photographers will pay incrementally
Viability Assumptions
Will it work for business?
  • We can price competitively
  • Unit economics work at scale
  • This increases retention, not just acquisition
Feasibility Assumptions
Can we build it?
  • We have the technical capabilities
  • Infrastructure can handle the load
  • We can ship in reasonable timeframe
Usability Assumptions
Can they use it?
  • Photographers can set up without training
  • Clients can navigate easily
  • Flow feels seamless with existing features

3 How I Would Test the Hypotheses

The goal is to run the smallest experiment that gives us the evidence we need to move forward with confidence. Start with the fastest, cheapest tests before investing in building.

üß™ Experiment Menu (Fastest ‚Üí Slowest)

Hours

One-Question Survey

"How painful is gallery delivery on a scale of 1-10?"
Send to 500 users, get signal in 24 hours.

Hours

Data Mining

What % of users have Pixieset/ShootProof integrations? Check our own analytics for behavioral signals.

Days

Customer Interviews

"Tell me about the last time you delivered a gallery..." 5-10 interviews can reveal clear patterns.

Days

Unmoderated Usability Test

Show prototype, watch them try to use it. UsabilityHub, Maze, or similar tools.

1-2 Weeks

Smoke Test / Fake Door

"Coming Soon: Imagen Galleries" ‚Üí measure clicks and waitlist signups. Real behavioral data, not opinions.

2-4 Weeks

Wizard of Oz

Manually deliver the experience to 10 users. Understand the real workflow before building anything.

4-8 Weeks

Concierge MVP

Build minimal UI with heavy manual backend. Validate value before investing in automation.

2-4 Weeks

Clickable Prototype

High-fidelity prototype for usability testing. Test usability assumptions before feasibility.

Guiding Principles

‚òÖ Research Principles

These principles guide every decision in the discovery process.

1. Problem First, Solution Second
Avoid solutioning before deeply understanding user pain. The capability emerges from the research‚Äîit's not the starting point.
2. Triangulate Data Sources
No single data point should drive decisions. Combine quantitative analytics, qualitative interviews, and behavioral experiments.
3. Test Cheap Before Building Expensive
Fake doors and painted doors before engineering. The goal is to fail fast and learn cheap.
4. Segment the Research
Wedding photographers ‚â† Portrait photographers ‚â† Commercial photographers. Each segment has different needs and workflows.
5. Measure Behavior, Not Just Opinions
What users do > What users say. Behavioral evidence trumps stated preferences every time.
6. Set Kill Criteria Upfront
Define what failure looks like before we're emotionally invested. Know when to pivot or kill an idea.

The Bottom Line

The methodology isn't about finding the right answer in a research project. It's about building an ongoing capability to continuously discover and validate opportunities‚Äîso that "what should we build next?" becomes a question we can answer with confidence, every quarter, forever.
