# UX Research Plan: Imagen AI

## Research Overview

This document outlines a comprehensive UX research approach for understanding Imagen AI's product, users, and market position from a user experience perspective.

---

## Research Objectives

### Primary Objectives
1. **Understand user needs and pain points** in professional photography workflows
2. **Evaluate product usability** and identify friction points in the current experience
3. **Assess user satisfaction** and measure the effectiveness of AI-powered editing solutions
4. **Identify opportunities for improvement** in workflow, features, and user experience
5. **Understand competitive positioning** from a user perspective

### Secondary Objectives
1. Map the complete user journey from discovery to adoption
2. Understand how photographers integrate Imagen into their existing workflows
3. Identify barriers to adoption and retention
4. Explore unmet needs and feature gaps
5. Validate value proposition and messaging effectiveness

---

## Research Phases

### Phase 1: Discovery & Understanding (Weeks 1-4)
**Goal:** Build foundational understanding of users, context, and current state

### Phase 2: Evaluation & Validation (Weeks 5-8)
**Goal:** Test current product experience and validate assumptions

### Phase 3: Optimization & Innovation (Weeks 9-12)
**Goal:** Identify improvement opportunities and test new concepts

---

## Research Methods

### 1. User Interviews

**Purpose:** Deep qualitative understanding of user needs, motivations, and pain points

**Participants:**
- **Primary:** Professional photographers using Imagen AI (10-15 participants)
  - Mix of wedding, portrait, event, commercial photographers
  - Various experience levels with the product (new users, power users)
- **Secondary:** Photographers not using Imagen AI (5-8 participants)
  - Competitor users
  - Non-AI editing tool users
  - Potential adopters

**Key Research Questions:**
- What does a typical editing workflow look like?
- What are the biggest pain points in photo editing?
- How did you discover Imagen AI?
- What were your initial expectations vs. reality?
- How has Imagen changed your workflow?
- What keeps you using (or not using) Imagen?
- What features are missing or could be improved?
- How do you feel about AI editing vs. manual editing?
- What concerns do you have about AI-powered editing?

**Interview Structure:**
- Pre-interview survey (demographics, usage patterns)
- 60-90 minute semi-structured interviews
- Show-and-tell: Ask participants to walk through their workflow
- Task-based scenarios: Observe them using the product

**Deliverables:**
- Interview transcripts and analysis
- User personas
- Journey maps
- Pain point prioritization matrix

---

### 2. Usability Testing

**Purpose:** Identify usability issues and measure task completion effectiveness

**Test Scenarios:**
1. **First-time user onboarding**
   - Creating a Personal AI Profile
   - Understanding how to train the AI
   - First editing session

2. **Core workflows**
   - Editing a wedding gallery (100+ photos)
   - Culling a photo set
   - Applying and adjusting AI Profiles
   - Using advanced features (masking, retouching)

3. **Integration workflows**
   - Importing from Lightroom
   - Syncing with Creative Cloud
   - Exporting edited photos

4. **Profile management**
   - Switching between Personal and Talent Profiles
   - Creating multiple profiles for different styles
   - Adjusting profile settings

**Methodology:**
- **Moderated remote sessions** (Zoom, Lookback, or similar)
- **Unmoderated testing** (UserTesting.com, Maze)
- **In-person sessions** (if possible, for power users)
- **A/B testing** for specific features or flows

**Metrics:**
- Task completion rate
- Time to complete tasks
- Error rate and type
- System Usability Scale (SUS) scores
- Net Promoter Score (NPS)

**Participants:**
- 5-8 users per scenario
- Mix of new and experienced users
- Different photography specialties

**Deliverables:**
- Usability test reports
- Issue prioritization (severity Ã— frequency)
- Video clips of critical issues
- Recommendations for fixes

---

### 3. Survey Research

**Purpose:** Quantitative validation of qualitative findings and broader user sentiment

**Survey Types:**

#### A. User Satisfaction Survey
- **Frequency:** Quarterly
- **Sample:** All active users
- **Metrics:**
  - Overall satisfaction (CSAT)
  - Feature satisfaction ratings
  - Likelihood to recommend (NPS)
  - Perceived value
  - Time saved perception

#### B. Onboarding Survey
- **Timing:** After first 100 edits
- **Sample:** New users (first 30 days)
- **Focus:**
  - Onboarding experience
  - Initial value perception
  - Barriers to continued use
  - Feature discovery

#### C. Workflow Survey
- **Frequency:** Bi-annually
- **Sample:** Active users
- **Focus:**
  - Current workflow patterns
  - Tool usage frequency
  - Integration with other tools
  - Pain points in workflow

#### D. Competitive Analysis Survey
- **Frequency:** Annually
- **Sample:** Professional photographers (users and non-users)
- **Focus:**
  - Tool awareness
  - Feature comparison
  - Switching behavior
  - Perceived strengths/weaknesses

**Deliverables:**
- Survey reports with statistical analysis
- Trend analysis over time
- Segmentation analysis
- Actionable insights dashboard

---

### 4. Analytics & Behavioral Research

**Purpose:** Understand actual user behavior through product usage data

**Key Metrics to Track:**

#### Engagement Metrics
- Daily/Monthly Active Users (DAU/MAU)
- Session frequency and duration
- Feature adoption rates
- Time to first value
- Retention cohorts

#### Workflow Metrics
- Average photos edited per session
- Profile creation and usage rates
- Culling vs. editing usage
- Cloud storage usage patterns
- Integration usage (Lightroom sync)

#### Performance Metrics
- Time saved per session
- Edit accuracy (re-edit rates)
- Profile training completion rates
- Feature discovery rates

#### Friction Points
- Drop-off points in onboarding
- Feature abandonment
- Support ticket analysis
- Error rates and types

**Tools:**
- Product analytics (Mixpanel, Amplitude, or similar)
- Heatmap tools (Hotjar, FullStory)
- Session replay tools
- Custom event tracking

**Deliverables:**
- Analytics dashboards
- Funnel analysis reports
- Cohort retention analysis
- Feature usage reports
- Behavioral segmentation

---

### 5. Competitive Analysis

**Purpose:** Understand competitive landscape and positioning

**Competitors to Study:**
- **Direct competitors:** Other AI photo editing tools
  - Luminar Neo
  - Topaz Photo AI
  - Adobe Lightroom AI features
- **Indirect competitors:** Manual editing workflows
  - Traditional Lightroom workflows
  - Preset-based workflows
  - Outsourced editing services

**Research Activities:**
1. **Feature comparison matrix**
   - Side-by-side feature analysis
   - Pricing comparison
   - Integration capabilities

2. **User reviews analysis**
   - App store reviews
   - Community forums
   - Social media sentiment
   - Review sites (G2, Capterra, etc.)

3. **Competitive usability testing**
   - Test competitor products with users
   - Compare workflows and experiences
   - Identify strengths and weaknesses

4. **Market positioning analysis**
   - Messaging comparison
   - Value proposition analysis
   - Brand perception

**Deliverables:**
- Competitive feature matrix
- SWOT analysis
- Positioning map
- Opportunity identification

---

### 6. Contextual Inquiry / Field Studies

**Purpose:** Observe users in their natural environment

**Activities:**
- **In-studio visits** with professional photographers
- **Remote observation** via screen sharing during actual work
- **Workflow shadowing** to understand real-world usage

**Focus Areas:**
- How photographers actually use the tool in context
- Integration with other tools and processes
- Workflow interruptions and context switching
- Environmental factors affecting usage
- Collaboration patterns (if applicable)

**Deliverables:**
- Field study reports
- Workflow diagrams
- Contextual insights
- Environmental considerations

---

### 7. Diary Studies

**Purpose:** Understand long-term usage patterns and evolving needs

**Duration:** 2-4 weeks

**Participants:** 10-15 active users

**Activities:**
- Daily/weekly check-ins
- Photo journaling of workflow
- Usage logging
- Reflection prompts

**Focus:**
- How usage patterns change over time
- Evolving needs and expectations
- Workflow adaptations
- Satisfaction over time

**Deliverables:**
- Diary study synthesis
- Usage pattern analysis
- Longitudinal insights

---

### 8. Card Sorting & Information Architecture

**Purpose:** Understand mental models and improve navigation

**Activities:**
- **Open card sort:** How users organize features
- **Closed card sort:** Validate proposed IA
- **Tree testing:** Test navigation structures

**Focus Areas:**
- Feature discoverability
- Menu organization
- Settings and preferences structure
- Profile management structure

**Deliverables:**
- IA recommendations
- Navigation improvements
- Feature organization insights

---

### 9. A/B Testing & Experimentation

**Purpose:** Validate design decisions and optimize conversion

**Key Areas for Testing:**
- Onboarding flow variations
- Feature discovery mechanisms
- Pricing page variations
- Profile creation process
- CTA placements and messaging
- Tutorial and help content

**Methodology:**
- Statistical significance thresholds
- Multivariate testing where appropriate
- User segmentation in tests

**Deliverables:**
- Test results and analysis
- Winning variations
- Insights for future tests

---

### 10. Accessibility Research

**Purpose:** Ensure product is usable by all photographers

**Activities:**
- **WCAG compliance audit**
- **Accessibility testing** with users with disabilities
- **Keyboard navigation testing**
- **Screen reader testing**
- **Color contrast analysis**

**Deliverables:**
- Accessibility audit report
- Compliance recommendations
- Inclusive design improvements

## User Segments

### Primary Segments

1. **Wedding Photographers**
   - High volume editing needs
   - Time-sensitive deliverables
   - Style consistency critical

2. **Portrait Photographers**
   - Lower volume, higher quality focus
   - Strong personal style emphasis
   - Client-specific adjustments

3. **Event Photographers**
   - Very high volume
   - Quick turnaround needed
   - Consistent style across events

4. **Commercial Photographers**
   - Client-specific requirements
   - Brand consistency needs
   - Professional quality standards

### Secondary Segments

5. **Real Estate Photographers**
   - Specific editing requirements
   - Volume and efficiency focus

6. **Newborn/Family Photographers**
   - Soft, natural style preferences
   - Client relationship focus

### Phase 3: Optimization
- What features are most/least valuable?
- How can we improve the onboarding experience?
- What would increase user retention?
