Research Methodology

Research Philosophy

I would use a triangulated research approach combining quantitative data, qualitative insights, and behavioral signals. The goal is to validate both the problem (do photographers actually struggle with this?) and the solution (would our specific approach solve it?).

Key Principle: Latent Needs vs. Stated Needs

Users don't request gallery/payment features because they've already solved these problems with other tools. This is a latent need‚Äîthey don't know they want it until they see it integrated. Our research must uncover these hidden opportunities, not just respond to feature requests.

Experiment Designs

Experiment 1: Fake Door Test

Setup

  • Add banner in Imagen AI dashboard: "Coming Soon: Deliver galleries & get paid‚Äîall in Imagen"
  • Track click-through rate to waitlist signup page
  • Capture email + short survey (what tools do you currently use?)
  • A/B test different value propositions
Variant Message Hypothesis
A (Control) "Coming Soon: Gallery Delivery" Baseline interest
B (Time Savings) "Deliver galleries in 1 click‚Äîno more switching tools" Time savings resonates
C (Payment Focus) "Get paid faster with integrated invoicing" Payment pain is primary
D (All-in-One) "Edit ‚Üí Deliver ‚Üí Get Paid‚Äîall in Imagen" Integration is the value

Experiment 2: Prototype Usability Test

Setup

  • Build clickable prototype of gallery delivery + payment flow
  • Recruit 15-20 users for moderated testing sessions
  • Assign tasks: "Deliver this gallery to your client and send an invoice"
  • Measure task completion, time, errors, satisfaction

Task Completion Rate >90% Observation + screen recording

System Usability Scale (SUS) >80 Post-task survey

Risk Mitigation

Risk: Users won't switch from existing tools

Mitigation: Focus on seamless integration value. Make the "edit ‚Üí deliver" flow so smooth that switching is the path of least resistance. Offer migration tools from Pixieset/ShootProof.

Risk: Payment processing is complex

Mitigation: Partner with Stripe Connect rather than building from scratch. Leverage their compliance, fraud prevention, and global coverage.

Risk: Feature bloat dilutes core value

Mitigation: Keep editing/culling excellence as the foundation. New features are optional add-ons, not replacements. Maintain support quality.

Risk: Competitive response

Mitigation: Move fast (6-month timeline). Our advantage is the integrated workflow‚Äîcompetitors would need to build editing AI to match.
